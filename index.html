Program In Action
Program In Action

Army Cold Weather Hand Warmer (CWHW).

The Engagement

A funded M&D partnership with DEVCOM Soldier Center.

Envision partnered with DEVCOM Soldier Center on a Manufacturing & Development program to design, prototype, and iterate the Cold Weather Hand Warmer, a mission-critical piece of soldier gear. The project translated a government requirement into a production-ready prototype inside Envision's Innovation & Prototyping Laboratory.

What It Demonstrates

A rapid prototyping partner, not just a manufacturing vendor.

Envision moves from requirement to prototype to production-ready output inside a DoD program structure. That posture, transition partner rather than subcontractor, is how the company enters federal conversations today.

The Arc That Matters

How verified capability becomes a federal engagement.

CWHW is not a one-off. It is a template. The same capability-to-engagement arc is how Envision enters every federal opportunity going forward, including the ones that this room might bring.

Replicate the pattern.

Strategic Direction
Strategic Direction

Where Envision is intentionally diversifying.

Envision is building the next chapter around four directional themes. Specifics are walked through in the room. What follows is the shape of the direction.

Theme 01
Federal and enterprise channel expansion.
Thoughtful work on how Envision funds, scales, and shows up inside both government and enterprise channels. Built for durability, not for a single program.
Theme 02
Innovation Lab as a funded R&D engine.
A posture that keeps the Innovation Lab engaged as a transition partner across research and commercial opportunities, rather than an internal cost center.
Theme 03
Portfolio diversification.
Extending core manufacturing and prototyping depth into adjacent capability areas where Envision's infrastructure, workforce, and compliance posture translate cleanly.
Theme 04
Enterprise partnerships.
Building a small set of enterprise relationships where manufacturing depth, federal channel access, and workforce advantage create durable value. Partner-led, not vendor-led.
