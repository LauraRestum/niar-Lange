Program In Action
Program In Action

Army Cold Weather Hand Warmer (CWHW).

The Partnership

Envision and DEVCOM Soldier Center, on a funded M&D program.

Envision partnered with DEVCOM Soldier Center on a Manufacturing & Development (M&D) program to deliver the Cold Weather Hand Warmer, a mission-critical piece of cold-weather soldier gear developed for the U.S. Army.

What Envision Owned

Design iteration, prototyping, and production-ready output.

Work was executed inside Envision's Innovation & Prototyping Laboratory, with Envision owning design iteration, prototype builds, and production-ready output. Envision operates as a transition partner inside DoD program structures, not as a subcontractor.

The Replicable Pattern
The Arc That Matters

How verified textile capability becomes a federal engagement.

Envision has already demonstrated a model of taking existing textile and sewn-goods depth, aligning it to mission need, supporting funded customer engagement, and positioning for broader transition. The same arc is how Envision diversifies the textile line into every new federal opportunity, including the ones this room might bring.

Step 01

Start with verified textile depth.

Decades of textile and sewn-goods manufacturing, backed by compliance infrastructure and innovation lab investment, create the foundation for every diversification move.

Step 02

Position as a transition partner.

Enter the conversation with an M&D posture, the same posture used with DEVCOM Soldier Center, rather than a subcontractor posture.

Step 03

Land inside a funded program.

Active M&D engagement on the Cold Weather Hand Warmer (CWHW) with DEVCOM Soldier Center, funded prototype work delivered into the DoD ecosystem.

Step 04

Replicate, and diversify the line.

Each new engagement extends the textile line into the next mission-adjacent vertical, including the collaborations being discussed today.

Strategic Direction
Strategic Direction

How Envision is diversifying the textile line.

Envision is building the next chapter of its textile vertical around four directional themes. Each one compounds an existing win. Specifics are walked through in the room; what follows is the shape of the direction.

Theme 01
Federal channel expansion for the textile line.
Active expansion of the DLA AbilityOne Tailored Logistics Support lane and supply access to 160+ stores nationwide, anchored by 16 Envision-operated Base Supply Centers in 10 states, into a structured federal go-to-market posture for textile and sewn-goods programs.
Theme 02
Innovation Lab as the textile R&D engine.
The Innovation and Prototyping Laboratory is positioned as a funded textile transition partner, with active DEVCOM Soldier Center engagement on cold-weather soldier gear as the current anchor.
Theme 03
Textile portfolio diversification.
Extending textile and sewn-goods depth into smart textiles, embedded sensors, and e-textile capability as part of a deliberate capability roadmap built on the existing production line.
Theme 04
Textile program partnerships.
A small, selective set of enterprise and research relationships that extend the textile line into new missions where federal credibility, configuration depth, and workforce advantage combine into durable joint value.
